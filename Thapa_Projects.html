Hydrogen Solar Cell Car

November 2019 - Principles of Engineering
Click here to view our full documentation

Me and my team of 4 worked to create a car powered only from hydrogen or solar cells. We applied our skills of simple machines, and energy to create an efficient car through vex parts as well as a vex motor. The 3 wheeled car car has a big to small gear ratio to maximize speed. The largest front wheel is spun though our gears and motor, dragging the back two wheels. For our car, we used only solar energy as we found hydrogen cells to add too much weight. Overall our speed was 4.35 seconds in one meter. While designing and testing our car, we encountered many challenges. One of them was finding away to manage our limited space that was given to us. We found it difficult to fit in two solar panels while making a longer and less wide car. Personally, on this project I had the role of designing the main base of the car and building the power sources. Overall, I have learned a lot from this project, most importantly, the process of trial and error and the perseverance involved when designing something.

Seating Chart Project

September 2021 - Computer Science Applications
Click here to view our full documentation

Me and my group of developers worked on an interactive gameboard project, programmed on Greenfoot. Using our knowledge from Runestone Javascript, we successfully programmed a game that consist of 40 tiles, two dice and 10 questions. When the user rolls the two dice and lands on a question tile, a popup presents a multiple choice question based on Runestone Units 10-12. The objective of the game is to try and reach the end in as few turns as possible One of the biggest challenges we had was creating a movement algorithm that would automatically turn the player, we figured it out by storing the previous direction and checking it with the possible future directions Another challenge we had dealt with was removing the popup screen after the question was answered, initially we had the code to “listen” for object interactions outside of the act method
